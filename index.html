Dennis Ritchie

The inventor of the C programming language

Dennis Ritchie, though he never received his PhD, contributed great things in the form of the C programming language and the UNIX operating system.

A timeline of his life:

  • 1941: Born in Bronx-ville, New York
  • 1967: Began working at Bell Labs Computer research
  • 1968: Defended his PhD Thesis, however was never awarded his PhD
  • 1969: Started work on the C programming language
  • 1983: Received Turing award for development of general operating systems theory
  • 1990: Received IEEE Hamming medal
  • 1997: Both Ritchie and Ken Thompson were made Fellows of the Computer History Museum, "for co-creation of the UNIX operating system, and for development of the C programming language."
  • 2011: Died at age 70
